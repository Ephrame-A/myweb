Shambel Abebe Bikila

The first Olympic gold medalist

Short biography of Shambel Abebe

  • Born on August 7, 1932, in the small community of Jato, Shewa.
  • Around 1952, he joined the 5th Infantry Regiment of the Imperial Guard.
  • served in the Kagnew Battalion during Korean War and rose to the rank of shambel (captain)
  • In 1956, Abebe finished second in the Ethiopian Armed Forces championship.
  • In 1960 he became the first ‘black African’ to win a gold medal at the Olympics, running barefoot in the marathon in Rome.
  • He won a second Olympic gold medal in Tokyo in 1964 by breaking world record
  • On March 22, 1969, Abebe was paralysed due to a car accident.
  • In 1973 he died in hospital following a brain haemorrhage
